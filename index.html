--- Work ---

Research //

I have worked at OHSU in the Casey Eye Institute Elks Informatics lab since 2011. My research group focuses on finding innovative ways to use retro active data, captured in Electronic Health Record systems, to drive meaningful changes in health care efficiency. My published works can be found on the NIH website.

Programming //

I am novice programmer just beginning to navigate the vast world of coding. Over the past few weeks at Epicodus I've been making silly website in order to practice using the programming languages HTML and CSS. My projects have ranged from animal montages, to a dream trip in Berlin, to a fictional cup cake shop website entitled EpiCupCakes.

Non-Profit //

Since 2015 I've been serving as a board member on Portland Ultimate board of directors. As a board member I strive to serve as a resource promoting growth in the sport of Ultimate and instilling the spirit of sportsmanship at all levels of play. In 2016 I hopes to help expand playing and volunteer opportunities to young adults in the community.

My name is Sarah Read-Brown. I grew up in Minnesota and moved to Oregon in 2011 to attend school at Lewis & Clark College where I studied Economics. I've worked at Oregon Health & Sciences University as a researcher since I graduated in 2011. In my role I specialize in picking out interesting data for presentations an making visually compelling displays of information. I am taking evening classes at Epicodus because I've because I am considering a career change and I believe programming will be a versatile and valuable skill to have as I re-enter the job market.


Outside of work I love playing Ultimate Frisbee. I have played Ultimate in Portland for the past 9 years in college, club, and Portland Ultimate Leagues. I currently play and captain Portlands elite womens team, Schwa, and I am passionate about the development of womens and girls ultimate. I am an avid amateur chef and enjoy cooking big meals for my friends and family. This past winter I have been obsessed with making soups and pies using seasonal ingredients. In the future I dream of traveling to India and spending months eating my way around the country while learning how to cook the local cuisine.
